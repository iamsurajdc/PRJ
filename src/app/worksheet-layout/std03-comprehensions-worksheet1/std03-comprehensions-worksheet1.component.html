Learning about The Pyramids

Why were pyramids built?

Pyramids were built as burial places and monuments to the Pharaohs (they are Egyptian kings). As part of their religion, the Egyptians believed that the Pharaoh needed certain things to succeed in the afterlife. Deep inside the pyramid, the Pharaoh would be buried with all sorts of items and treasures that he may need to survive in the afterlife.

How big were the pyramids?

There are around 138 Egyptian pyramids. Some of them are huge. The largest, is the pyramids of Khufu, also called the Great Pyramid of Giza. When it was first built, it was over 480 feet tall! It was the tallest man-made structure for over 3800 years and is one of the Seven Wonders of the World. It is estimated that this pyramid was made from 2.3 million blocks of the rock weighing 5.9 million tons.

Fun Facts about The Pyramids
  • The Great Pyramid of Giza points very precisely to the north.
  • The pyramids of Egypt were all built to the west of the River Nile. This is because the western side was associated with the land of the dead.
  • The base of a pyramid was always a perfect square.
  • They were built mostly of limestone.
  • There were traps and curses put on the tombs and the pyramids, to try and keep robbers out.
